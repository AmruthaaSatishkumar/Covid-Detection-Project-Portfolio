A system for the detection of COVID-19 & its severity  using Chest X-Rays as well as Patient Health Records.

The system takes Chest X-Rays (Posterior Anterior view) and the health
records of a patient as input. A deep learning model will then process these inputs to
predict whether the patient has COVID or not and in the case that the patient tests positive,
the model will also try to predict the severity of the case by looking for adverse effects of
the virus on the lungs. The proposed model takes into consideration the problems with
other diagnostic tools such as time and cost and tries to overcome them. View More

Need? Considering the worldwide spread
of the pandemic and the high cost of testing, there is a need for a
faster and cheaper way to test for the virus.

How it helps? Early detection of the virus and its severity can also impact the outcome of life or death of  a person and go a long way in decreasing the mortality rate and help control the spread of
this pandemic.

How are we different? The proposed product overcomes all the limitations of the testing methods. It makes
predictions within 10 seconds , is very cheap , and is also able to make predictions about the
severity of the case making it more actionable

Model Used for Classifying Covid pos/neg

The final output X-ray image is fed to the training model. We applied transfer learning , where the VGG 16 model acts as the base , to train our dataset.On top of the base model, we applied additional layers to obtain a training accuracy of 98%

Analysis of Patient Electronic Health Records

The preprocessed data was put through multiplee models such as Multiple Linear Regression,Logistic Regression, Decision Tree, Pruned decision tree,Random Forest, Boosted random forest,Neural networks, and a 3-layer CNN model. After comparing the performance of all the models, Boosted Random Forest was the best performer with accuracy of 96%

Severity Prediction

What's the story behind our product?

In the face of the global COVID-19 pandemic, my two friends and I embarked on a remarkable journey to create a solution that would streamline the testing process. Our project aimed to predict whether an individual was COVID positive or negative by harnessing the power of chest X-rays and Electronic Health Records (EHRs). We envisioned a world where COVID testing could be made easier and more efficient, ultimately helping to save lives.

Driven by a passion to make a tangible impact, we sought guidance from a knowledgeable relative who happened to be a doctor. This invaluable expert enlightened us about the profound effects COVID has on chest opacity, information that could be deciphered through chest X-rays. With this newfound understanding, we set out to leverage the opacity in chest X-rays as a means to determine the severity of the disease.

We firmly believed that our project had the potential to revolutionize the real world, especially considering the existing limitations surrounding COVID at that time. Our goal was to address the pressing need for faster and more accurate testing, allowing medical professionals to make critical decisions promptly and efficiently.

However, as with any ambitious undertaking, we encountered certain limitations along the way that prevented our project from reaching fruition in the real world. Despite these setbacks, we remained resolute in our belief that our efforts had not been in vain. Through this project, we amalgamated existing knowledge with innovative techniques, unearthing impactful results that illuminated new possibilities for future endeavors.

Though our project may not have materialized as we initially envisioned, the knowledge gained and the strides made during its development have laid a solid foundation for future advancements. We remain hopeful that our work will inspire and serve as a catalyst for further innovation in the realm of COVID testing, ultimately contributing to the ongoing fight against this relentless global health crisis.

Our journey may have encountered hurdles, but our dedication, resilience, and unwavering belief in the power of knowledge continue to drive us forward. Together, we can conquer the challenges posed by COVID-19, paving the way for a safer and healthier future for all.
